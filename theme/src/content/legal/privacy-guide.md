# Privacy Policy — Getting Started

A Privacy Policy is legally required in almost every jurisdiction if you collect **any** personal data — including names and email addresses from a contact form, IP addresses from server logs, or analytics cookies.

> **⚠️ Disclaimer:** This guide provides starting points and links to official resources. It is not legal advice. Always consult a qualified legal professional in your jurisdiction.

---

## 🇩🇪🇦🇹🇨🇭 DACH Region (Germany, Austria, Switzerland)

A "Datenschutzerklärung" is mandatory under GDPR/DSGVO. It must be accessible from every page of your website (typically via a footer link).

* **[Datenschutz-Generator.de](https://datenschutz-generator.de/)** (Dr. Thomas Schwenke): The gold standard for German-language privacy policies. Very detailed configuration options covering analytics, contact forms, newsletter tools, and hosting providers.
* **[e-recht24.de — Datenschutzerklärung](https://www.e-recht24.de/muster-datenschutzerklaerung.html)**: Free privacy policy generator for German websites. Covers common use cases.
* **[WKO.at — Datenschutz](https://www.wko.at/service/wirtschaftsrecht-gewerberecht/eu-dsgvo-datenschutz-grundverordnung.html)**: Austrian Chamber of Commerce GDPR guidance and templates.
* **[EDÖB (Switzerland)](https://www.edoeb.admin.ch/)**: Swiss Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner. Provides guidance on the revised Swiss Data Protection Act (revDSG) which took effect Sept 2023.

## 🇬🇧 United Kingdom

The UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018 apply to all businesses processing personal data. There is **no** small business exemption.

* **[ICO — Privacy Notice Generator](https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/uk-gdpr-guidance-and-resources/privacy-notices/)**: The Information Commissioner's Office provides a free, step-by-step tool to create a compliant privacy notice tailored to your business.
* **[ICO — Lawful Basis Checker](https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/uk-gdpr-guidance-and-resources/lawful-basis-interactive-guidance-tool/)**: Interactive tool to determine the correct legal basis for each of your data processing activities.
* **[ICO — SME Web Hub](https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/sme-web-hub/)**: Dedicated resources designed specifically for small businesses and sole traders.
* **[Termly.io](https://termly.io/)**: Generates UK GDPR-compliant privacy policies with automatic updates when regulations change.
* **[TermsFeed.com](https://www.termsfeed.com/)**: Quick template-based generator with UK-specific options.

## 🇮🇪 Ireland

Ireland is the EU lead supervisory authority for many major tech companies, and the DPC provides excellent general guidance.

* **[DPC — Data Protection Commission](https://www.dataprotection.ie/)**: Provides [Guidance for SMEs](https://www.dataprotection.ie/) with checklists and practical steps.
* **[DPC — Know Your Obligations](https://www.dataprotection.ie/en/organisations/know-your-obligations)**: Step-by-step guide to GDPR obligations including privacy notices.
* **[Enterprise Ireland](https://www.enterprise-ireland.com/)**: Provides business-focused data protection guidance for Irish companies.

## 🇺🇸 United States

The US has no single federal privacy law, but a rapidly growing patchwork of state laws. If you have users in these states, you may need to comply:

### Key State Privacy Laws

| State | Law | Threshold | Key Requirement |
|:------|:----|:----------|:----------------|
| **California** | CCPA/CPRA | $25M revenue, or 100K+ consumers | "Do Not Sell" link, detailed privacy policy |
| **Virginia** | VCDPA | 100K+ consumers | Opt-out rights, privacy policy |
| **Colorado** | CPA | 100K+ consumers (no revenue floor!) | Universal opt-out signals, privacy policy |
| **Connecticut** | CTDPA | 100K+ consumers (lowering to 35K in July 2026) | Opt-out rights, consent for sensitive data |
| **Utah, Texas, Oregon, Montana, Delaware, Iowa, Tennessee, Indiana, New Hampshire, New Jersey, Nebraska, Kentucky, Maryland, Minnesota, Rhode Island** | Various | Varies by state | Privacy policies with state-specific disclosures |

### Resources

* **[FTC — Privacy & Security](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/privacy-security)**: Federal Trade Commission guidance and enforcement actions.
* **[California AG — CCPA](https://oag.ca.gov/privacy/ccpa)**: Official CCPA/CPRA guidance, regulations, and FAQ.
* **[IAPP — US State Privacy Legislation Tracker](https://iapp.org/resources/article/us-state-privacy-legislation-tracker/)**: The most comprehensive and regularly updated tracker of all US state privacy laws.
* **[Termly.io — State Privacy Laws Overview](https://termly.io/resources/articles/us-state-privacy-laws/)**: Practical overview with compliance guidance.
* **[Future of Privacy Forum](https://fpf.org/)**: Non-profit think tank with excellent analysis of emerging US privacy legislation.

## 🇨🇦 Canada

* **[OPC — Privacy Guide for Businesses (PIPEDA)](https://www.priv.gc.ca/en/privacy-topics/privacy-laws-in-canada/the-personal-information-protection-and-electronic-documents-act-pipeda/pipeda_compliance_help/guide_org/)**: Official guide from the Office of the Privacy Commissioner. Covers the 10 fair information principles.
* **[OPC — Privacy Guide for Small Businesses: The Basics](https://www.publications.gc.ca/site/eng/9.825227/publication.html)**: Specifically designed for SMEs and freelancers.
* **[Quebec — Law 25 (Bill 64)](https://www.cai.gouv.qc.ca/)**: Quebec's privacy law has stricter requirements than PIPEDA — consult the Commission d'accès à l'information if you operate in Quebec.

## 🇦🇺 Australia

* **[OAIC — Privacy for Small Business](https://www.oaic.gov.au/privacy/privacy-for-organisations/small-business)**: Guidance from the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner. Small businesses (under $3M turnover) are generally exempt, but **check the exceptions** (health services, trading in personal information, government contracts).
* **[OAIC — APP Quick Reference](https://www.oaic.gov.au/privacy/australian-privacy-principles)**: Summary of the 13 Australian Privacy Principles.
* **[business.gov.au — Privacy Obligations](https://business.gov.au/)**: Official government resource.

---

## 🇳🇿 New Zealand

* **[OPC — Privacy Act 2020 Guide](https://www.privacy.org.nz/)**: New Zealand's Privacy Act has **no small business exemption**. All businesses must comply.
* **[OPC — Privacy Self-Assessment](https://www.privacy.org.nz/tools/)**: Interactive tools for assessing your privacy compliance.
* **[digital.govt.nz — Privacy Statements](https://www.digital.govt.nz/)**: NZ government guidance on writing privacy statements for websites.

---

## 🇸🇬 Singapore

* **[PDPC — Personal Data Protection Commission](https://www.pdpc.gov.sg/)**: Administers the PDPA. Provides [Advisory Guidelines](https://www.pdpc.gov.sg/guidelines-and-consultation) and a Data Protection Trustmark certification.
* **[PDPC — Guide to Data Protection for SMEs](https://www.pdpc.gov.sg/)**: Practical guide for small businesses.

---

## 🇿🇦 South Africa

* **[Information Regulator](https://inforegulator.org.za/)**: Enforces POPIA (Protection of Personal Information Act). Provides compliance templates and guidance for responsible parties.
* **[POPIA Compliance Framework](https://popia.co.za/)**: Community resource with practical compliance guidance.

---

## 🇮🇳 India

* **[MeitY — Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023](https://www.meity.gov.in/)**: The DPDP Act and 2025 Rules introduce consent-led, rights-based data protection. Privacy notices must be in clear, plain language.
* **[DPDP Rules 2025](https://www.meity.gov.in/)**: Check for the latest implementation guidance — the rules are being phased in.
