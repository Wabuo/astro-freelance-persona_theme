# Legal Notice / Impressum — Getting Started

A Legal Notice (called "Impressum" in German-speaking countries) identifies who is responsible for the content of your website. In many jurisdictions this is **mandatory** for any commercial or semi-commercial website.

> **⚠️ Disclaimer:** This guide provides starting points and links to official resources. It is not legal advice. Always consult a qualified legal professional in your jurisdiction.

---

## 🇩🇪🇦🇹🇨🇭 DACH Region (Germany, Austria, Switzerland)

An **Impressum** is legally mandatory for virtually all websites in these countries (§5 TMG / §25 MedienG / Art. 3 UWG). Even personal blogs with any commercial intent (ads, affiliate links) require one.

* **[e-recht24.de — Impressum Generator](https://www.e-recht24.de/impressum-generator.html)**: The standard free generator for German websites. Covers GmbH, UG, freelancers, and sole traders.
* **[Datenschutz-Generator.de](https://datenschutz-generator.de/)** (Dr. Thomas Schwenke): Highly respected, lawyer-built generator. Also offers Datenschutzerklärung generation.
* **[WKO.at — Impressum Guide](https://www.wko.at/service/wirtschaftsrecht-gewerberecht/Impressumspflicht.html)**: Official Austrian Chamber of Commerce guide with templates.
* **[SECO (Switzerland)](https://www.seco.admin.ch/)**: Swiss Secretariat for Economic Affairs — guidance on e-commerce obligations including Impressum.

---

## 🇬🇧 United Kingdom

The UK has no "Impressum" requirement per se, but the **Companies Act 2006**, **Electronic Commerce (EC Directive) Regulations 2002**, and **UK GDPR** together require clear identification of the business operator on your website.

* **[ICO — Information Commissioner's Office](https://ico.org.uk/)**: The UK's data protection authority. Offers a [Privacy Notice Generator](https://ico.org.uk/for-organisations/uk-gdpr-guidance-and-resources/privacy-notices/) and lawful basis checker.
* **[GOV.UK — Business Names](https://www.gov.uk/running-a-limited-company/signs-stationery-and-promotional-material)**: Official guidance on what must appear on your website (company name, registration number, registered address, VAT number).
* **[Termly.io](https://termly.io/)**: Generates UK GDPR-compliant legal pages including disclaimers and terms of use.
* **[TermsFeed.com](https://www.termsfeed.com/)**: Offers UK-focused template generators for multiple legal documents.

---

## 🇮🇪 Ireland

Ireland follows EU GDPR and the **E-Commerce Regulations (SI 68/2003)**. Websites must identify the service provider, geographic address, and registration details.

* **[DPC — Data Protection Commission](https://www.dataprotection.ie/)**: Ireland's GDPR supervisory authority. Provides [SME Guidance](https://www.dataprotection.ie/) and checklists.
* **[Citizens Information — Business Websites](https://www.citizensinformation.ie/)**: Official guidance on legal requirements for Irish websites.

---

## 🇺🇸 United States

There is no single federal "legal notice" law, but various state and federal requirements apply depending on your business:

* **[FTC — Federal Trade Commission](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/privacy-security)**: Enforces transparency requirements under Section 5 of the FTC Act. Provides small business guides.
* **[California Attorney General — CalOPPA](https://oag.ca.gov/privacy)**: California's Online Privacy Protection Act requires any website collecting personal information from California residents to post a privacy policy.
* **[SBA — Small Business Administration](https://www.sba.gov/)**: Federal resource for small business legal obligations and compliance.
* **[Termly.io — US State Privacy Tracker](https://termly.io/resources/articles/us-state-privacy-laws/)**: Excellent overview of which US states have comprehensive privacy laws and how they affect your website.

## 🇨🇦 Canada

* **[OPC — Office of the Privacy Commissioner](https://www.priv.gc.ca/en/privacy-topics/privacy-laws-in-canada/the-personal-information-protection-and-electronic-documents-act-pipeda/pipeda_compliance_help/guide_org/)**: Official PIPEDA guidance for businesses. Includes a privacy guide for SMEs.
* **[Government of Canada — Business Obligations](https://www.ic.gc.ca/)**: Industry Canada guidance on e-commerce and disclosure obligations.

---

## 🇦🇺 Australia

* **[OAIC — Office of the Australian Information Commissioner](https://www.oaic.gov.au/)**: Administers the Privacy Act 1988. Provides a privacy self-assessment tool for small businesses. Note: businesses under $3M turnover are generally exempt unless they handle health data or trade in personal information.
* **[business.gov.au](https://business.gov.au/)**: Official government resource for website legal requirements.

---

## 🇳🇿 New Zealand

* **[OPC — Office of the Privacy Commissioner](https://www.privacy.org.nz/)**: Administers the Privacy Act 2020. There is **no** small business exemption — all businesses collecting personal data must comply.
* **[digital.govt.nz](https://www.digital.govt.nz/)**: New Zealand government digital services guidance.

---

## 🇸🇬 Singapore

* **[PDPC — Personal Data Protection Commission](https://www.pdpc.gov.sg/)**: Administers the PDPA. Provides advisory guidelines and a Data Protection Trustmark certification scheme.

---

## 🇿🇦 South Africa

* **[Information Regulator (South Africa)](https://inforegulator.org.za/)**: Enforces POPIA. Provides compliance guidance and templates for responsible parties.

---

## 🇮🇳 India

* **[MeitY — Ministry of Electronics & IT](https://www.meity.gov.in/)**: Administers the Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act 2023 and its 2025 Rules. Guidance is evolving rapidly — check for the latest Rules updates.

## ⚖️ How to Replace This Guide

1. Create a markdown file in `src/content/legal/` (e.g., `src/content/legal/disclaimer.md`).
2. Paste your custom legal text into it.
3. Update your `freelance-persona.config.ts`:

    ```typescript
    legal: {
      // ...
      disclaimer: "disclaimer" // The ID of the file in src/content/legal/
    }
    ```
